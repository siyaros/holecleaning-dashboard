Calculation Methodology

The excess cuttings volume is calculated by comparing the theoretical gauge hole volume (if hole stayed at bit size) with the actual hole volume (using caliper measurements).

Gauge Volume = π × (bit_radius)² × interval_length × (1 - porosity)
Actual Volume = π × (caliper_radius)² × interval_length × (1 - porosity)
Excess = Actual Volume - Gauge Volume

The (1 - porosity) factor accounts for the fact that only solid rock becomes cuttings — pore space doesn't contribute to cuttings volume.
